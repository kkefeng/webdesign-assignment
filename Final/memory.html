I had an amazing time exploring Berlin, Germany. The city is full of history and culture.
Berlin is a bustling metropolis that has something for everyone: history, culture, art, nightlife and shopping. Berlin is home to some of the most famous museums in the world including the Pergamon Museum and Humboldt Forum. There are also plenty of historic buildings such as the Berliner Dom or TV Tower.

I was pleasantly surprised by how much there is to see and do in Berlin! I only wish I had more time there to explore more of the museums, parks, cafes, restaurants, shops, etc. Berlin is a city of contrasts. The city offers a wide range of things to do and see, from historic buildings and museums, to the nightlife scene.
Kuala Lumpur is a city that has been transformed by modernization and development. It is a place where you can find the best of both worlds, modernity and tradition. The city is also known for its beautiful parks, friendly people and delicious food. With this in mind, we have created a list of things to do in Kuala Lumpur that will show you what the city has to offer.

1) Visit the Batu Caves:
This Hindu temple complex with caves is one of the most famous attractions in Kuala Lumpur. The caves are located about 12 km from the heart of Kuala Lumpur and are home to a large statue of Lord Murugan (a Hindu deity). It takes about an hour to explore all the caves, but there are plenty of shops around if you need something or want to grab lunch before heading back down.

2) Visit KLCC:

The Easter Island is a Chilean island in the southeastern Pacific Ocean, at the southeasternmost point of the Polynesian Triangle. It is famous for its 887 extant monumental statues, called moai, created by the early Rapa Nui people.

The island has some very interesting history, which has been preserved through the oral tradition of its people and their artworks that they have left behind. Most of these are carved on rocks or wood and have been found all over the island. One such example is a large stone statue called Moai which stands at 13 feet tall and weighs 82 tons! This statue would have taken many months to carve out using only stone tools!
